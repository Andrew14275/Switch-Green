‚ö° Understanding Renewable Energy Sources

‚òÄÔ∏è

Solar Energy

How it works: Solar panels (photovoltaic cells) convert sunlight directly into electricity. India receives abundant sunlight year-round, making solar the fastest-growing renewable source.

Current Capacity: 129.92 GW
Best States: Rajasthan, Gujarat, Karnataka
Home Use: Rooftop panels (1-10 kW systems)
üí®

Wind Energy

How it works: Wind turbines convert kinetic energy from moving air into electricity. India's long coastline and windy regions make wind power the second-largest renewable source.

Current Capacity: 53.60 GW
Best States: Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Maharashtra
Offshore Potential: 70+ GW along coastlines
üíß

Hydroelectric Energy

How it works: Flowing water turns turbines to generate electricity. Small hydro projects (under 25 MW) use river flow without large dams, providing consistent baseload power.

Current Capacity: 5.16 GW
Best Regions: Himalayan states, Western Ghats
Advantage: 24/7 reliable generation
üåæ

Biomass Energy

How it works: Agricultural waste, crop residue, and organic matter are converted into electricity. This solves waste management problems while generating power, especially beneficial for farming states.

Current Capacity: 11.61 GW
Best States: Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra
Benefit: Reduces stubble burning pollution
